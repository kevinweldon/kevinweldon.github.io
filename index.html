What I'm Up To

  • Currently
    I'm in a computational mechanics master's program at the Technische Universität München. I spent two months between my third and final semesters backpacking New Zealand and have returned for my second summer in Germany to start my thesis. The long title is "Reducing corner singularities in soil mechanics with the Wave-Based Method and special purpose enrichment functions," but in more general terms, it's looking into new and efficient methods for investigating ground-borne vibrations and creating highly convergent numerical models. But most of the time you can find me running on the Isar or hanging with friends at the Englischer Garten.
  • Before All That
    I started this master's program in October 2023. In the years between my undergrad and now, I worked for a couple years as an engineer at a manufacturing company near my hometown and for a couple more years at a nuclear energy company in the city. Both were great experiences with good mentors and gave me a sweet array of steel toes, field work, engineering sales, service consulting, and mechanical simulations.
